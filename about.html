   HAZEL ELSBACH

I am inspired by a variety of visual, tactile, and emotional moments that provoke my interest. When I work I try to catch my own attention, by excavating my psyche for the ideas and instincts buried in it. Often my work will come to look like an archeological site or map, with pieces of intense detail scattered amid large portions of uncharted space.

I work primarily with the mediums of printmaking, photography, drawing, and painting, and am interested in exploring the line between three-dimensional and two-dimensional space. I often look to science, biology, nature, dance, and so-called domestic crafts such as patchwork and embroidery, as subjects to explore. I am also drawn toward collage, which allows me to incorporate found objects and borrowed imagery into drawings and prints.

My aesthetic choices are tied to the materials and actions I decide to engage with. Juxtaposition between obsessive intricacy, void-like expanses, repetitive motion, and loose marks are recurring themes. I am fascinated with the chemistry between artist and materialâ€”often there are times when the material I am using guides me more than I control it. This give and take of control is at play in the construction of every human made object, and my awareness of it is particularly heightened when I am making work.

My working process is often so intimate that it becomes self-referential. A vocabulary of marks and symbols become integrated organically, so that I am constantly discovering new appearances. I look for compositions that combine structure with ambiguity, decorative elements with stark minimalism, anxiety with serenity; images that feels disparate but whole, and unfinished yet complete.
